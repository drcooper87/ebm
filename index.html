July 2013: EBM awarded U.S. Department of Energy funding

This project is part of a three-year, $7.04 million US Department of Energy grant to advance next-generation, energy-efficient manufacturing processes. EBM is to model the energy, carbon and cost benefits of incremental sheet forming. Led by Ford, other collaborators include Northwestern University, The Boeing Company, and Penn State Erie.

Dan Cooper Post-doctoral Researcher

My current research is on evaluating the potential energy, carbon and cost savings associated with the successful development of flexible sheet metal forming processes. This research is in collaboration with Ford and Boeing. I did my undergraduate and masters degrees in mechanical engineering at the University of Cambridge (UK). I worked as a navel architect for one year before returning to Cambridge to complete a PhD investigating sustainability in the steel and aluminium industries.
